Las Vegas and Probability

Most people think of Las Vegas as the epitome of glitz and glammer: America's Adult Playground. They picture slot machines, exploding volcanoes and a light beam that shoots into space. But, this page is about something truly exciting: the underlying Math and Probabilty that make Las Vegas "America's Math Playground" (not an official title).

Vegas Style Video Poker

Maximizing profit in Las Vegas is as dependant on exploiting the gambler's fallacy (a misuse of the Law of Large Numbers) as it is on boutique hotels and showgirls. Every new game that goes on the casino floor, every wrinkle to the rules of blackjack or roulette, and every new slot machine that gets placed has been thoroughly vetted by statisticians who calculate the exact amount of profit to be made for every dollar wagered in the long run (a correct use of the Law of Large Numbers, or Bernoulli's Theorem).

Probability has been connected to gambling since its inception. In fact, Classical Probability Theory came about when two scientists (Pascal and Fermat) were presented a question about a gambling problem.

Gambling, Probability & Vegas Timeline

  • 3000+bc - First known usage of dice, found in Egyptian tombs and other ancient locations across the world.
  • 1400s - Baccarat is created, making it one of the oldest casino games.
  • 1524 - Gerolamo Cardano writes the first mathematical treatment of probability, although it was not published until 1633.
  • 1601 - An early version of Blackjack called ventiuna (21) is referenced in a book by Miguel de Cervantes Saavedra, the author of Don Quixote.
  • 1638 - The Ridotta, a gambling house and the forefather of the modern casino is opened in Venice, Italy. Amazingly, it is still in operation today, making it the world's oldest casino.
  • 1654 - Blaise Pascal and Pierre de Fermat begin a correspondence to analyze a gambling problem. This discussion is considered the birth of Classical Probability Theory.
  • 1657 - Christian Huygens, a Dutch scientist, publishes the first book on probability, entitled De Ratiociniis in Ludo Aleae. It focuses on probability as it relates to gambling. The book is a big success and, eventually, leads to probability becoming a rigorous mathematical discipline.
  • 1713 - Jacob Bernoulli writes The Art of Prediction, which introduces a number of principles including the Law of Large Numbers (hence Bernoulli's Theorem).
  • 1796 - Roulette, as we know it today, appears in Parisian gaming houses.
  • 1829 - Poker appears in New Orleans. While the provenance of the game is murky, this is the first known appearance of the game in the United States.
  • 1891 - The first slot machines appear in New York.
  • 1910 - The US outlaws all gambling in the US as part of the larger Prohibition movement
  • 1931 - Nevada legalizes gambling. With gambling illegal in the rest of the country, Las Vegas soon becomes the center of gambling in the United States.
  • 1946 - The Flamingo Hotel, the first luxury hotel on the strip opens. While an initial failure, it opens the door for many more destination resorts in Vegas. "The West's Greatest Resort Hotel" costs $6 million and houses 105 rooms.
  • 1989 - The Mirage casino opens on the strip. At the time, it is the world's largest and most expensive hotel, sporting 3,044 rooms and a construction cost of $630 million. The era of the Vegas mega-resort continues to this day.
